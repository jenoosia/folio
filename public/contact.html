I love music, and I love photography.

(They go along very well together, don't you think?)

I started shooting live music when I moved to Singapore in 2012, but I've been taking pictures (with a humble little compact) since 2008. Singapore has a small but very vibrant local music scene- plus an awesome international music scene. I've always been a music nerd, and ever since I first photographed a band rockin' out with the bass thrumming across my entire body, I've never left the front row. It's been fun and challenging, and every gig has left a mark on my lens and my ears (tinnitus be damned!).

Drop me a line at jensen.ching (at) gmail (dot) com.

I use a Nikon D7000 with the following lenses: 11-16mm f2.8, 24-70mm f2.8, 80-200mm f2.8.

I walk around the 'hood with an Olympus E-P5 with a 17mm f.18 and a 45mm f1.8.

I love my trusty old Lumix LX3. It's just been retired, but it takes amazing shots for a point-and-shoot camera.
